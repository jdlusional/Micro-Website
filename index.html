The work, broadly

What I do, across domains

a1Research & scholarship+
Doctoral research at Vanderbilt on what drives nonprofit and organizational success, grounded in governance theory, organizational identity, and mixed-methods analysis. The throughline is a measured, evidence-based way of asking why some institutions thrive while others struggle.
b1Policy analysis+
Nonpartisan analysis at the Texas Capitol, currently as Lead Finance & Economics Analyst with the House Research Organization, and previously as Lead Local Government Analyst with the Senate Research Center. The work turns complex legislative questions into clear, usable analysis.
a2Consulting+
Through MICRO Group, LLC, strategic and operational consulting for institutions in the nonprofit, education, and government sectors, spanning management, integration, culture, research, and operations.
b2Writing & ideas+
On the Nature of Things, an essay publication on policy, philanthropy, and the discipline of thinking carefully in public. The title borrows from Lucretius; the subject is the business of thinking for a living.
