Selection methodology and scoring scale

Issues are selected and ranked using an objective seven-indicator scoring methodology applied uniformly across all topics, drawn exclusively from official public sources. Each issue receives a composite score out of 18 points; indicators A through E each score 0 to 3, indicator F scores 0 to 1, and indicator G scores 0 to 2. The composite score, individual indicator scores, factual basis, and linked official sources appear under each issue's selection criteria. For the full structure of the methodology, including a per-indicator description of what is measured and how scores are assigned, see the Methodology Explained section.

Bills referenced

Because Texas convenes biennially, no 90th-session bills exist yet, and filing opens in November 2026. The measures listed under each issue are 89th-session vehicles, including companions, that were enacted, vetoed, or left unfinished, and represent the most likely starting points when the legislature reconvenes.

Methodology Explained

Expand to view the full scoring framework

Purpose and design

This briefing applies a uniform, objective scoring framework to every candidate issue rather than ranking by editorial judgment. The intent is to make selection and ordering reproducible: any analyst working from the same official sources should arrive at the same composite score for each issue. The framework draws exclusively from publicly available legislative records and does not incorporate polling, advocacy materials, or anticipated political momentum.

Sources

Indicator scoring relies on the following primary sources, in order of use:

  • Texas Legislature Online (capitol.texas.gov) for bill text, filing volume, companion identification, committee assignments, action history, and final status.
  • Speaker of the House and Lieutenant Governor interim charges for the cycle preceding the 90th session.
  • Governor proclamations, emergency item designations, and special session calls.
  • Legislative Budget Board fiscal notes and appropriations records for fiscal magnitude scoring.
  • Texas Constitution and Secretary of State election results for constitutional amendment activity.

The seven indicators

Each issue is scored across seven indicators. Indicators A through E each score 0 to 3, indicator F scores 0 to 1, and indicator G scores 0 to 2, for a maximum composite of 18 points. Scoring is point-based and discrete: each indicator score reflects a defined factual threshold, not a subjective weighting.

Indicator A. Interim Charges (0 to 3)

Measures whether the presiding officers of either chamber formally directed legislative committees to study the topic during the preceding interim. Scoring reflects the number of charges issued and whether both chambers, or only one, took up the topic. A score of 3 indicates substantial and bicameral interim study activity.

Indicator B. Bill Volume (0 to 3)

Measures the number of distinct bills filed on the topic during the most recent regular session, including House and Senate companions. High filing volume signals broad legislative interest. Scoring thresholds reflect the topic's relative position in the session's overall filing pattern.

Indicator C. Governor Designation (0 to 3)

Measures whether the Governor formally elevated the topic through a State of the State emergency item designation, a special session call, or a proclamation directing legislative action. Emergency item designation and special session inclusion carry the highest weighting because they alter the legislative calendar and procedural rules.

Indicator D. Prior Session Carryover (0 to 3)

Measures whether the topic was substantively considered in the previous regular session but left unresolved, partially enacted, vetoed, or returned through litigation. Scoring distinguishes between fully resolved topics, partially resolved topics, and topics where major vehicles were filed but did not pass.

Indicator E. Fiscal Magnitude (0 to 3)

Measures the scale of state funds appropriated, dedicated, or projected to be required for the topic, as documented in fiscal notes, the General Appropriations Act, or constitutionally dedicated revenue streams. The scoring reflects order of magnitude rather than precise dollar figures.

Indicator F. Constitutional Amendment (0 to 1)

A binary indicator. Scores 1 if the topic involves a constitutional amendment placed on the ballot or proposed as a Senate or House Joint Resolution in the prior session, and 0 otherwise. The narrow range reflects that constitutional activity is either present or absent, not graduated.

Indicator G. Bipartisan Engagement (0 to 2)

Measures whether bills on the topic attracted joint authorship or coauthorship from members of both major parties, and whether floor votes crossed party lines. The narrow range reflects that bipartisan engagement is a supplementary signal rather than a primary driver of legislative attention.

Composite score and ranking

The composite score is the simple sum of the seven indicator scores. Issues are ranked in descending order of composite score. Ties are broken by indicator C (Governor Designation), then by indicator A (Interim Charges), reflecting the principle that executive and committee priority signals are the strongest predictors of floor activity.

What the scores do and do not represent

The composite score reflects the volume and authority of the institutional signals pointing toward an issue at the time of publication. It does not predict passage, forecast political outcomes, or measure public importance. An issue can score high and fail to pass; an issue can score low and become consequential during session. The framework is descriptive of the legislative landscape entering the 90th session, not predictive of its outcomes.

Updates

Scores are current as of the publication date. As pre-filing opens in November 2026 and as committees report through the interim, individual indicator scores may shift. Material changes will be reflected in subsequent editions of this briefing.
