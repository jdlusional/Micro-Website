Research

Focus & background

My research asks a deceptively simple question: why do some institutions succeed where others, with similar resources, struggle? The work sits at the meeting point of organizational theory, governance, and applied data analysis.

Doctoral capstone

What Drives Success for Nonprofits in America? A National Framework is my doctoral capstone at Vanderbilt's Peabody College. It asks what distinguishes high-performing nonprofit boards from struggling ones.

Following the comparative, pattern-identification approach of Chua and Rubenfeld's The Triple Package, it pairs IRS Form 990 financial data with nationwide interviews to identify a small set of actionable, observable drivers of board effectiveness. The project is in its early conceptual stage, defining scope and building its theoretical foundation.

Background & current fellowship

My broader research spans nonprofit governance, organizational identity and culture, demography, and the use of quantitative methods in policy settings, including legislative applications of AI transcription analysis.

I am currently an NPA Fellow in Policy and Practice with Nonprofit Austin at Austin Community College, alongside prior research fellowships with UT Austin's Population Research Center and RGK Center for Philanthropy.
